# PubWeb Strategy Session — 2026-03-26

## Context

Following a Brookfield Comfort pricing session where everything is running well and waiting on data. Conversation turned to diversification and motivation.

## Current State

- **3 software products built**: LMS Local (football betting/fundraiser), MeetWithFriends (event organiser), PubWeb (pub websites)
- **PubWeb identified as strongest opportunity** — 1 paying customer (600/year), 1 more interested
- Next.js sites, easy for non-techies to update hours/menus/gallery without WordPress or an agency
- Can spin up a new site within days

## Key Decisions

- **PubWeb is the focus** — LMS Local and MeetWithFriends parked (not killed)
- **Positioning**: Not competing with website builders. Selling a service — independence from agencies, control over their own content, simplicity
- **Pricing**: 600/year (50/month) is right. Don't go lower. Consider a small setup fee (150-200) later
- **Sales approach**: Show, don't ask — build prototype sites using publicly available info, drop leaflets with live preview URLs
- **Batch size**: 10 at a time, iterate messaging, then scale

## Go-To-Market Plan

1. Pick 10 local pubs/cafes
2. Build a quick prototype for each using Google/Facebook info (hours, photos, menu)
3. Drop off a leaflet with their live preview URL — no pitch, no awkward conversation
4. Let the work speak — follow up only if they engage
5. Measure response, adjust messaging, repeat

## Other Opportunities

- A contact's nephew does app/website sales — potential rev-share or commission arrangement worth exploring
- Don't wait on that though — leaflet approach needs no salesperson

## Mindset Notes

- Strength is systems and building, not traditional sales — so build the sales process as a system
- The leaflet approach reframes sales as "showing your work" rather than "asking people to buy"
- Same iterative, data-driven approach as Brookfield pricing: test small, measure, adjust, scale
