# Ways of Working — Thinking Out Loud

This isn't a plan. It's a place to think about how work gets done, who's involved, and how to make it sustainable — not just productive.

## The Problem

Working solo with AI is fast. But fast isn't everything. Missing:
- Human interaction during the working day
- Someone to bounce ideas off who brings their own experience
- The energy of a shared room, even over coffee
- People learning the business alongside you
- Resilience — if you're the only one who knows, it's fragile

## What Works Today

- AI handles data, analysis, execution prep (pricing sessions, dashboards, scripts)
- Decisions stay with you — AI proposes, you decide
- Speed: a full IVES pricing review (33 changes across 10 colours) in one sitting
- No scheduling overhead, no context-switching

## What Could Work With People

### Roles that would add, not slow down
- **Ops support** — uploads, stock checks, FBA shipments, competitor screenshots. Frees you to think.
- **A peer** — someone in e-commerce to talk shop with. Doesn't need to be an employee. Regular coffee or call.
- **A learner** — someone growing into the business. Present in sessions, asking questions, gradually taking on decisions.

### How AI + humans could work together in a session
- AI prepares the data and summary before the meeting
- You and colleague(s) review the summary together — discuss, challenge, decide
- AI executes the agreed changes live
- Colleagues handle the manual follow-up (uploads, stock orders, competitor checks)

The speed doesn't have to go away. The prep and execution stay fast. The *decision-making* gets richer because there's another perspective in the room.

### Things to figure out
- What does "in the room" look like? In person? Screen share? 
- How do you involve someone without them feeling like they're just watching?
- What decisions benefit from a second opinion vs. which are routine?
- Is there a role that justifies part-time help now, or is this about finding a peer first?

2026-04-03: Started thinking about this. The IVES pricing session was productive but highlighted the gap. Enjoying the work, but missing the human side. 2026-04-03 (later): Key insight — humans don't belong *inside* the AI session. They belong around it. Morning standup (what are we doing today, what do you need from me), then solo AI work, then handoff (uploads, stock checks, monitoring). The meeting is the human bit. The AI session is the execution. Don't mix them — layer them.
