## Printing Approach

**Batch print generic leaflets** — no individual URLs on the leaflet itself. Print 50-100 at a time to keep costs down.

**Personalise with a handwritten note or sticker** attached to each leaflet:
> "I've built a preview for your business — have a look: nagshead.ofd.net"

A handwritten note feels more personal than a printed URL and signals someone actually took the time. This converts better than a mass mailshot feel.

## Industry Variants

Start with the **generic version** for all business types. Once you have paying customers across sectors, consider printing industry-specific batches with these swaps:

| Element | Pub/Cafe | Barber/Salon | Trades |
|---------|----------|--------------|--------|
| Headline | YOUR PUB ONLINE... | YOUR SALON ONLINE... | YOUR BUSINESS ONLINE... |
| Phone screen shows | menus, hours, photos | services, pricing, hours | services, areas, photos |
| Background imagery | pub/cafe interior | salon interior | local street / van |
| Example shown | pub customer | salon customer | trades customer |

Same back panel, same pricing, same contact — just the front panel swaps.

## The Note/Sticker

This is the personalisation layer. Options:

**Handwritten note** (best for small batches, most personal):
> Hi — I've built a preview website for [business name]. Have a look when you get a chance: [business].ofd.net
> No obligation, just wanted to show you what's possible.
> [Your name]

**Printed sticker** (better for larger batches via reps):
> We've built a free preview for your business:
> **[business].ofd.net**
> Have a look — no obligation.

Either way, the note does the heavy lifting. The leaflet explains what the service is. The note says "we already did it for you."
