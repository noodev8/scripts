# Online Front Door — Strategy Sessions

## Session 2 — 2026-03-26: Rebrand & Scale Model

### The Problem with PubWeb
Brookfield doing £250k with office, mall unit, and 2 staff. PubWeb at £50/month felt like a distraction — too narrow (pubs only), too manual (personal leaflet drops), and £30k ceiling didn't move the needle. Needed a model that could scale without personal legwork.

### Key Decisions
- **Rebranded from PubWeb to Online Front Door**
- **Parent company**: Noodev8 (noodev8.com) — Online Front Door is a Noodev8 product
- **Domains (to buy)**:
  - onlinefrontdoor.co.uk + onlinefrontdoor.com — brand and marketing site
  - ofd.net — customer subdomain platform (e.g. `inglenook.ofd.net`)
- **Market expanded**: pubs, cafes, coffee shops, barbers, salons, trades (plumbers, electricians, builders), takeaways, small gyms — any local business
- **Not selling a website** — selling an online presence, managed and simple
- **Upsell**: customers can upgrade to their own domain pointed at their site

### Positioning
> "Your business's front door, online."

You don't need to learn Wix. You don't need an agency. You get a professional online presence that you can update yourself from your phone — hours, services, photos, gallery. We set it up, you own it.

### What Makes This Different
- **Build first, show second** — most marketing says "we could build you a website." Ours says "we already did — look."
- **Not competing with website builders** — competing with having no presence, or being stuck with an agency for every small change
- **Millions of businesses already have a website, but millions don't.** That's the market.

### Commission Sales Model
- Straight commission, no employees
- Reps get 50% of first year revenue per conversion (£300 per customer)
- Full recurring revenue from year 2 onwards
- Contact's nephew does app/website sales — potential first rep

| Rep brings in | They earn (year 1) | You earn (year 1) | You earn (year 2+) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 customers | £3,000 | £3,000 | £6,000/yr |
| 30 customers | £9,000 | £9,000 | £18,000/yr |
| 50 customers | £15,000 | £15,000 | £30,000/yr |

### Phases
1. **Platform build** — Generalise the template for different business types (pub, barber, trades, cafe). Get site creation time down from 2 days towards under an hour.
2. **Manual sales** — Get the first customers done manually. Prove the process across different business types.
3. **Commission reps** — Get the structure right, then recruit reps. Give them a territory. Straight commission.
4. **Paid acquisition** — Mailshots and ads once the conversion process is proven. Build up the sales channel and funnel gradually.

Note: Automated prospecting (scraping Google Maps to generate previews at scale) is parked — not needed yet, can revisit when volume justifies it.

### Revenue at Scale
| Customers | Avg £/month | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| 50 | £50 | £30,000 |
| 100 | £50 | £60,000 |
| 200 | £65 | £156,000 |
| 500 | £75 | £450,000 |

Higher averages at scale assume upsells (own domain, premium features like booking/events).

## Session 1 — 2026-03-26: Original PubWeb Strategy

### Context
Following a Brookfield Comfort pricing session where everything is running well. Conversation turned to diversification and motivation.

### Original State
- 3 software products built: LMS Local (football betting/fundraiser), MeetWithFriends (event organiser), PubWeb (pub websites)
- PubWeb identified as strongest opportunity — 1 paying customer (£600/year), 1 more interested
- Next.js sites, easy for non-techies to update hours/menus/gallery
- Could spin up a new site within days

### Original Decisions (now superseded by rebrand)
- PubWeb was the focus — LMS Local and MeetWithFriends parked (not killed)
- Pricing: £600/year (£50/month)
- Sales approach: show, don't ask — build prototype sites, drop leaflets with live preview URLs
- A contact's nephew does app/website sales — potential commission arrangement worth exploring
