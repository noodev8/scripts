# Online Front Door — Nurture Process

A simple, repeatable system for turning outreach into paying customers — without ever feeling like you're selling.

## The Principle

Every interaction is about **the site**, never about the deal. You're showing your work, improving it based on feedback, and letting the business decide when they're ready. The Nags Head took several weeks and multiple iterations before signing up — that's normal, not a failure.

## Cadence

| Stage | When | What to do | Mindset |
|-------|------|------------|---------|
| **Drop** | Week 0 | Drop leaflet with live preview URL. No pitch. Walk away. | The site speaks for itself |
| **Check** | Week 1-2 | Pop back in casually. "Did you get a chance to look at the site?" | Just asking if they saw it, nothing more |
| **Improve** | Week 3-4 | If they engaged at all, make a visible improvement to their preview (new photo, service update, seasonal change). Mention it next time you're in, or text/email if you have contact details. | Shows you're invested, costs you nothing |
| **Ask** | Week 5-6 | If warm but not committed: "Is there anything you'd want it to do differently?" | Shifts from "buy this" to "help me build this for you" |
| **Pause** | After week 6 | If no movement, step back. Light check-in every 2-3 months. Don't chase. | Some come back months later — new manager, agency frustration, etc. |

## Rules

1. **Never ask "do you want to sign up?"** — always talk about the site, not the deal
2. **Every touch adds value** — an update, a question, useful info. Never just a "checking in" with nothing behind it
3. **Track every prospect** so nothing goes quiet without you noticing (the tracker below)
4. **Don't skip the improve step** — a small update to their preview is the most powerful move you have. It proves you care and reminds them the site is real
5. **Respect the pause** — if someone isn't interested, a quarterly check-in is enough. Circumstances change (new owner, website breaks, agency raises prices)

## Restarting a Quiet Conversation

When a prospect has gone quiet (like The Inglenook):

1. Make a small, visible improvement to their preview site
2. Drop in or message: "I made a couple of updates to your preview, have a look when you get a chance"
3. That's it — no pressure, no ask. The update restarts the conversation naturally

This works because it's not "just checking in" — you've actually done something for them.

## Rep Handoff

When commission reps are in place, they follow the same cadence. Provide them with:
- Access to the prospect tracker
- The ability to request preview site improvements from you (they sell, you build)
- Clear rules: never push, always lead with the site

### Stage definitions

- **New** — Leaflet/outreach done, no response yet
- **Seen** — They've looked at the preview or acknowledged the outreach
- **Warm** — Engaged in conversation, asked questions, showed interest
- **Quiet** — Were warm but have gone silent. Not dead — just paused
- **Paying** — Signed up
- **Not interested** — Explicitly declined. Quarterly check-in only

| Trades (plumber, electrician, builder) | Services, area covered, reviews, contact |
| Takeaways | Hours, menu, delivery info |
| Small gyms & studios | Classes, timetable, pricing |

## Measuring What Works

After each batch of 10 outreach attempts, note:

- How many looked at the preview? (analytics if set up, or just from conversation)
- How many reached the **Warm** stage?
- How many converted to **Paying**?
- What questions or objections came up?
- Which business types responded best?

Use this to iterate the leaflet, the preview sites, and the cadence — same approach as Brookfield pricing. Test small, measure, adjust, scale.
