### The two best emails and what they tell us

We looked at Andreas's best email (4.44% click rate) and Summer's email (the only Campaign 2 sale) side by side. They use completely different approaches:

**Andreas — 4.44% click rate, 0 orders:**
- Text only. No product images, no prices.
- Personal and short: "Hey Andreas, a batch of Birkenstock has just landed — and some of it is already reduced."
- One button: "VIEW LATEST BIRKENSTOCK"
- Works because you HAVE to click to see what's there. Curiosity drives the click.

**Summer — 1.11% click rate, 1 order (£40.39):**
- Product catalogue layout. 6 products with images, names, crossed-out prices.
- Multiple "Shop now" buttons.
- Works because the customer can see exactly what they're buying before they click. Fewer clicks, but the people who do click are ready to buy.

### The takeaway

Both approaches have value:
- **Curiosity** gets people to your site (high clicks)
- **Showing products** pre-qualifies buyers (clicks convert to orders)

---

## What to do for Campaign 3

**Try to combine both strengths in one email:**

1. **Keep it personal and short** — use the customer's name, conversational tone, 2-3 sentences max before the first CTA
2. **Create curiosity** — "we've just reduced prices", "sizes are going", "new stock just landed"
3. **Show 1-2 hero products** — not six, just your best picks with an image, price, and link. Give them a reason to click AND enough to buy when they land.
4. **Always set preview text** — this shows in the inbox next to the subject line. Martin left his blank last time.
5. **Keep subject lines short and curious** — "You might want to see this" beat "Ready for Sunny Steps? Birkenstock EVA from £34 – Flying Off Shelves!"
